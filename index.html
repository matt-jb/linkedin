Put yourself in their shoes:

A recruiter wants to find the best candidates with the least effort.

An employee wants his or her bonus money.

They'll cheer for you through the whole of the recruitment process - and possibly give good advice and help.

How?

First: invite a few people every working day

Got a premium account?

Add note:

"Hi [name], I'd be happy to stay in touch to exchange knowledge and ideas with interesting IT people :)"

Who to invite?

  • IT Recruiters
  • People working in your tech-stack
  • Employees of companies you're interested in

Second: write posts regularly

Once a month? Bi-weekly? Weekly? It's up to you. Just do it regularly.

But I don't have anything to write about!

  • A bug you needed to research before fixing
  • A tutorial you recently completed (or started)
  • A tech podcast episode you liked
  • Your favourite tool / framework / library
  • A YouTube video on an interesting IT topic
  • Tech book reviews
  • Showcase your projects, opinions, or insights
  • A conference you're going to
  • Career milestones (promotions, switching tech-stack, team changes)
  • A meetup you recently attended (and the handsome presenter giving a talk)

"All you have to do is document your journey."

- Taylor Desseyn, VP of Global Community @ Torc

Source: Syntax podcast

Tips!

Be regular

It takes time to build a reputation and a following

  • Be nice (It takes 20 years to build a reputation and five minutes to ruin it.)
  • Longer posts perform better
  • Avoid politics and other potentially heated subjects
  • If you're Polish, write in Polish
  • Post in the mornings (schedule posts if needed)
  • Want to put code in the image thumbnail? Use carbon.now.sh
  • Memes are fine
  • Personal posts are fine, but don't overdo it
  • Have an updated and carefully crafted profile
    • Professional profile picture
    • Highlighted posts
    • Appealing background/banner image
    • Skills section properly highlighted
    • Compelling "About you" section
    • Detailed descriptions of what you did in previous (and current) companies

Keyword-optimize your profile

"Take the information from my resume down below and create an about section for my LinkedIn profile. Include keywords that a recruiter would be looking for to hire a [name of job title]. [resume information]"

Source: Farah Sharghi

Tweak this method to your needs!

Freelancer looking for gigs (or side-gigs)?

The more connections you have, the more likely clients will reach out to you.

Want to switch tech‑stacks?

Invite developers and technical managers working in this tech stack.

Targeting few, specific companies?

Try highly personalized employee searches and messages.

Not afraid of losing a job?

This might open new doors, you didn't know existed.

Unexpected benefit

Organically getting more "cold calls" from recruiters.
